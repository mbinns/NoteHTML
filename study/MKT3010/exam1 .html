Chapter 1

Definitions

  • Marketing:
    • A set of processes for creating capturing communicationg and delivering value to customers and managing customer relationships in ways that benefit the org and its stakeholders
  • Marketing plan:
    • A written document composed of an analysis of the current marketing situation, opportunities and threats in terms of the 4 p's:
      • Product, price, place, promotion
  • Exchange:
    • Trade of things of value between the buyer and seller so that each is better off as a result
  • Market Mix:
    • Product, Price, Place, Promotion
  • Goods:
    • Items that can be touched
  • Services:
    • Intangable offerings
  • Business-2-Consumer:
    • Process in which companies sell to consumers
  • Business-2-Business:
    • The process of buying and selling goods or services to be used in the production of other goods and services for comsumption by the buying org or for resale by whlesalers and retailers
  • Consumer-2-Consumer:
    • Process in which consumers sell to other consumers
  • Relational Orientation:
    • Philosophy that buyers and sellers should develop a long term relationship
  • Customer Relationship Managment:
    • A business philosophy and systems that focus on building loyalty among the firms most values customers

Core aspects

  • Marketing helps create value
  • Marketing is not about satisfying customer needs and wants
  • Marketing entails an exchange
  • Marketing requires a product, price, place, promotion
  • Marketing can be performed by individuals and organizations
  • Marketing affects various stakeholders

Purpose

  • Creates value for customers, firm, partners, and society

Marketing Mix

  • 4 controllable characteristics that a firm uses to respond to the wants of its target markets

Price

  • Everything has a price, but it doesn't have to be a monetary price
  • Is everythings the buyer gives up:
    • Money
    • Time
    • Energy

Place

  • Represents all the things needed to get the product to the right customer when they want it
  • Place more specifically deals with retailing and marketing channel management:
    • Supply chain managment
      • Set of approaches and techniques that firms employ to efficiently and effectively intergrate their suppliers, manufacturers warehouses tores and other firms

Product

  • Must add/create value for the consumer
  • Many products combine goods and services
  • Two forms:
    • Goods:
      • Items that can be touched by the consumer
      • Example:
        • Can of coke
    • Services:
      • Intangable offerings that benifit the consumer typically produced by people or machines
      • Example:
        • Dry cleaning
    • Ideas:
      • Intellectual concepts, thoughts opinions and philosophiess
      • Example:
        • Go4zero

Promotion

  • Promotion is the communication by a marketer that informs, pursuades and reminds potential buyers about a product or service to influence their opinions and elicit a response
  • Makes people want to buy the product
  • Can increase a products value

Exchange

  • Sellers provide a product or service
  • Consumers give money and information to the seller

Equation

  • \(Value = Benifits\ Recieved - (Cost+Hassel)\)

Who

  • Can be preformed by individuals as well as businesses:
    • Individuals market themselves when applying for jobs
  • Businesses attempt to sell a good or service

Value Based Marketing

  • Focus on four activities:
    • They share information about their customers and competitors across their own org
    • Strive to balence customers benifits and costs
    • Concentrate on building relationships with customers
    • Take advantage of new tech
