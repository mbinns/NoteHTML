Cryptography

Basics

  • Clear text goes into encryption function
  • Results of encryption is cipher text
  • Decryption function can get the resulting clear text from the cipher text
  • If both the encryption and decryption function have the key this is called symmetric key
  • If the keys are different for both decryption and encryption then its called asymmetric key

Types

Cesar Cipher

  • Rotating letters in the alphabet by 'x' offset (the key)

Vigenere Cipher

  • A sequence of Cesar Ciphers strung together

DES

  • DES not used any more

Attacking

Cipher Text

  • Attacking cipher text you look for pattern repetition
  • For example in a Cesar Cipher you look for the letter repeated the most and use that as your key

Vigenere Cipher

  • A sequence of Cesar Ciphers strung together and to attack you looked for correlation of repeating letters based on index shifts
  • Example)
