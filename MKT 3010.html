Step 1: Business Mission

  • Mission Statement:
    • What is the purpose of the organization
    • Type of business
    • Goals and objectives to accomplish
    • Building blocks:
      • Sustainable Competitive Advantage

Competitive advantage:

  • V - Something of Value
  • I - Difficult to imitate
  • R - Rare in the marketplace
  • O - Organization can support it:
    • Difference between a competitive advantage and a sustainable advantage is if the organization can support it
  • Customer Excellence:
    • All about a company looking to build loyalty with the customers
    • Eddie Bauer is an example of a company that looks to bring customers back with their lifetime policy
  • Company Culture:
    • Clashing between upper management and new hires due to culture differences
    • This can make or break if you enjoy your job
  • Intellectual property:
    • Patents
    • Trademarks
    • Company secrets
  • Operational excellence:
    • This is, and is not a competitive advantage.
    • Its the result of a having a competitive advantage then then leveraging it again
      • For example: Having a low price attracting customers and then being able to negotiate with suppliers for an even lower price
  • Product Excellence:
    • You essentially establish an idea of the product being the best
    • When you are in situations where you have never bought a product before, you will be much more likely to buy a brand you recognize
  • Locational Excellence:
    • You need to be in an area where you product/service will sell
  • Social Responsibility:
    • Being socially responsible builds on a competitive advantage
    • You have to stick to being socially responsible because you have customers who pay attention

Step 2: Situational Analysis

  • Assessment of organization internal and external environment
  • Tool SWOT:
    • S - Strengths
    • W - Weaknesses
    • O - Opportunities
    • T - Threats

Step 3: IDing opportunities

  • Using STP:
    • Segmentation:
      • Divide the marketplace
    • Targeting:
      • Evaluate attractiveness
      • After segmentation, targeting is figuring out what segment are you going to go after
    • Positioning:
      • Define the marketing mix
      • After targeting, positioning is figuring out HOW you are going to market to the target group

Step 4: Marketing Mix

  • 4P's:
    • Product and value creation
    • Price and value capture
    • Place and value delivery:
      • Where and how customers get the product
    • Promotion and value communication:
      • Integrated marketing communications (IMC)
