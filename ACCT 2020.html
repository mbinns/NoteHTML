  • Predicting cost behavior:
    • Saying costs are linear is okay within a relevant range (Fixed cost)
    • Difference between fixed cost and variable cost is total variable cost
    • Total cost is total variable cost + fixed cost
    • Three categories
      • Variable
        • Total variable cost:
          • Linear relationship
        • Varies in direct proportion to changes in the level of activity
        • Variable cost per unit is constant
        • Cost drivers is a measure of whatever causes the incurrence of a variable cost
      • Fixed:
        • Remains constant in total regardless of changes in the level of activity
        • Example: Manufacturing facility that produces 10,000 widgets and property taxes are 2000$ property taxes are a fixed cost because it does not matter how much you produce the property taxes stay 2000
        • The more you produce the lower the fixed cost per unit, fixed cost/units:
          • 2000/10,000 widgets = .20cents a unit
        • Types:
          • Committed:
            • Typically cannot reduce or cut out these costs without doing harm to the company:
              • Example: Cutting back on research and development, in the short term it may be okay but it will set you back for the long run
              • Example: Stopping production on a product
            • Multiyear planning horizon, has long term effects
          • Discretionary:
            • You can often reduce the ammounts or even eliminate them with out doing harm to the company:
              • Example of a fixed cost would be the advertising budget
            • These are typically things that are voluntary for the company
      • Mixed:
        • Cost containing both mixed and varibale costs:
          • Example: Renting a car with a cost per day and a charge per mile:
            • The cost per day is fixed
            • The cost per mile is variable
        • Need to seperate the fixed portion from the variable portion because you want everything to fall into the fixed or variable categories
        • How to break them appart:
