Chapter 1 Managerial Account and Cost Concepts

8/17/16

  • What is Managerial Accounting:
    • Designed to provide info to people within the business
  • Segments:
    • Geographical area
    • Type of business
    • Different business segments:
      • Look to see how well each segment is doing
  • There are no standards that are mandatory in managerial accounting
  • Three vital activities:
    • Planning (Budgeting)
    • Controlling:
      • Feedback and Feedforward mechanisms
      • Performance reports:
        • What should have happened with the budgeted figures in a period of time
        • What did happen with the budgeted figures in a period of time
        • Sometimes an unfavorable variance is not a negative but it is still flagged on a PR
    • Decision making:
      • Selecting a course of action from competing alternatives
      • Making data driven decisions
  • Professional Certs:
    • Certified Management Accountant (CMA)
    • Certified Public Accountant (CPA)
    • Certified Internal Auditor (CIA)

8/18/16

  • Six business management perspectives that go beyond the numbers:
    • Ethics perspective:
      • Code of conduct for management
      • IMA:
        • Institute of management accountants ethical code
      • General guidelines:
        • Maintain a high level of professional competence
        • Treat sensitive matters with confidentiality
        • Maintain personal integrity
        • Disclose information in a credible fashion
      • What should be done if an individual finds evidence of ethical misconduct:
        • You should report the issue to each higher level of authority
    • Stategic management perspective:
      • A game plan that enables a company to attract customers
      • Distinguishes themselves from the competition
      • Customer intimacy:
        • Fancy hotel
      • Operational Excellence:
        • Wallmart cheepness
      • Product leadership:
        • Rolex
    • Risk management:
      • Every strategy has a risk
      • This is the process of anticipating the risks and control the risk or mitigate the impact to processes
    • Corperate social responsibility:
      • When making a decision you need to take into account the needs of all the stakeholders and how a decision would impact them
    • Process management:
      • A business process is a series of steps that are folowed in order to carry out some task
      • Assembly line is a good example because is addes value in the form of uniformity and a streamlined process
      • Value chain is used to describe how an organization functional departments interact with one another
      • JIT (Just in time production) dont make the product untill you get the order
      • LEAN+ and ITIL Processes
    • Leadership perspective:
      • Intrinsitc motivation:
        • Something within themselves that motivates them
      • Extrinsic motivation:
        • External motivation factors
      • Cognitive Bias:
        • You have a belief and things that are not consitant with your beleifs you tend to avoid
  • The numbers matter even though there are no rules because the internal decision makers cannot make a well informed decision
  • Some companies will give up the bottom line to meet the perspectives they outline:
    • They will not cut too much profit otherwise they would go out of business
