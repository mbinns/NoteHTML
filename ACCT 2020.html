  • EXAM 29th
  • Cost classifications:
    • Assigning costs to cost objects:
      • These are groupings of costs that are similar in origin
    • Account for costs in manufacturing companies
      • What ever you are making is a cost object
    • Cost objects:
      • Could be the dept of the company
      • The division of the company
      • Some cost objects can be a direct cost to one object and also an indirect cost to another object
      • Anything you want to cost (Product/Service)
  • Manufacturing company examples
    • 3 types of inventory:
      • Raw materials:
        • Just because it is a raw material does not mean it is not a finished good:
          • For a tire company a tire is a finished good
          • For a car company a tire is a raw material
      • Work-in-Process/Progress:
        • Traditional production lines are a good example of this
          • Traditional - things at the start are raw materials and things at the end is a finished product
          • Anything inbetween the start and the end is a work in progress
      • Finished goods:
        • Anything that is ready to be sold for that particular company
  • Financial statement preperation:
    • Internally prepared using contribution format:
      • If you produce more than what you are selling then your net income will go down because it is reported as an expense
    • Externally prepared using absorbtion/full:
      • You can predict net income by predicting production compared to sales
        • You can increase net income by producing more than you sell because it is reported as an asset
  • Predicting Cost Behavior:
    • Cost behaviors are looked at in straight line replationships in managerial accounting
    • In a fixed range (Called relavent range) the costs are "fixed" meaning that they are in a straight line
  • Making decisions:
    • Should you increase company size
    • Should you decrease
    • Should you remove a product or make more of them
  • Cost objects:
    • Direct costs are a cost that can be easily and conveniently tracked to a source (Cost object):
      • Example: A cost object is widget the people who are making the widgets salary is a direct cost
    • Indirect cost is the inverse of direct cost where is cannot be traced to a specific cost object:
      • Example: The supervisor for the people making the widget is an indirect cost
      • Example: if the widgets are being charged to the department then the workers salary is still a direct cost of the widget, but now the supervisors salary is now also a direct cost
  • Categories of Cost objects:
    • Manufacturing costs:
      • Raw materials:
        • Direct cost
      • Direct labor:
        • Direct cost
      • Manufacturing overhead:
        • Indirect cost because you don't know how to distribute the cost of a building to each production piece
        • If it involves the making of the product then it falls under this:
          • Example: Supervisor of workers in the factory falls in this category
      • Prime costs:
        • Cost of direct materials + direct labor
      • Conversion Cost:
        • Direct labor cost + manufacturing overhead
    • Nonmanufacturing costs:
      • Selling costs:
        • Order getting and order filling:
          • Paying sales person commision
          • Pay shipping to customer
      • Administrative costs:
        • Paying the accountant
        • Does not involve the making of the product:
          • Example: Supervisor for accountants falls under this
