  • Three basic principals:
    • Voltage:
      • Described as potential energy
      • Some kind of battery that has electric potential where current will flow from high to low
      • Volts
    • Current:
      • Flow of electricty from high to low
      • Amps
      • Electrons flow negative to positive, but we think of current as going positive to negative
    • Resitance:
      • Resists the flow of electricity
      • Ohms
      • Resitance decreases the amperage flowing through:
        • Ohms Law is the measurement of this
  • Ohms Law:
    • V=IR
    • Voltage=CurrentxResitance
  • Power:
    • P=IV
    • Power=CurrentxVoltage
  • Why does ohms law matter:
    • Because you need to know how long a battery is going to last
    • And because some components act as resisters
    • Diodes:
      • Restirct the flow of current in one direction
      • Typically have resisters in series to limit the current to the diaoded to stop it from blowing out
