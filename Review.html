Chapter 4

  • Absorption Costing:
    • All manufacturing costs both fixed and variable are assigned to units of product. Units are said to fully absorb manufacturing costs
    • Most countries, including the united states require some form of absorption
  • Job Order Costing:
    • Using in situations where many different products are produced each period.
    • Costs are traced back to the job and then divided by the number of units produced to arrive at an average cost per unit
  • Computing predetermined Overhead Rates:
    • Manufacturing overhead is an indirect cost, meaning that it is either difficult or impossible to trace it
    • Some costs in manufacturing overhead are variable and some are fixed
    • Allocation is used to assign overhead costs to products:
      • Allocation Base is a measure such as direct labor hours or machine hours that is used to assign overhead costs to products 
      • Predetermined Overhead = Estimated total manufacturing overhead cost
                        ---------------------------------------------
                         Estimated total amount of allocation base
    • Predetermned overhead is calculated before the period begins (using estimates)
      • Follows a 4 step process:
        • Estimate the allocation base
        • Estimate total fixed manufacturing overhead cost
          • OR Estimate variable manufacturing costs
        • Use Y=a+bX:
          • Y = estimated total manufacturing cost
          • a = estimated total fixed costs
          • b = estimated total variabe costs
          • X = allocation base
        • Divide by allocation base to get predeturmined overhead by unit
  • Applying manufacturing overhead 
    • Overhead per job = Predetermined Rate X Allocation base for job
    • This is called a normal cost system
