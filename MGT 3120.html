Decision Modeling

3 Parts

  • Must get step one correct before you have the ability to get the other two steps correct because they both depend on the DV variable
  • If you are not using what you used in step one in step 2 and 3 then what you have in step 1 is wrong
  • Decision Variables (DVs):
    • Represent the decisions to be made
    • Question to ask yourself
      • "What do I need to know"
  • Objective Function(OF):
    • Measure of performance in terms of DVs
    • Question to ask yourself
      • "Why are we solving this problem"
  • Constraints:
    • Express restrictions on the values that can be assigned to the DVs
    • Question to ask yourself
      • "What restrictions must we work under"

Linear Programming (LP)

  • Type of decision model
  • Characteristics:
    • Must be a decision to be made
    • DVs can take on any values*
      • Constraints express the restrictions on the values that can be assigned to the DVs
    • OF either minimizes or maximizes the measure of performance
    • OF and Constraints must be linear in form:
      • Example) X+Y<=100
  • invalid linear examples:
    • No exponents
    • No multiplication of variables:
      • Constants can be multiplied against a variable:
        • 2X+3Y is valid
        • XY is not valid
    • No Roots
    • No division of variables

Solutions

  • A solution is any choice of values for the decision variables:
    • No constraints placed on the values
  • Feasible Solution is a solution that satisfies all of the constraints:
    • Must meet ALL constraints
    • Example:
      • 10/10 constraints
  • Infeasible solution is a solution that doesn't satisfy all of the constraints:
    • Fails at least one constraint
    • Example:
      • A solution that meets 9/10 constraints is still infeasible
  • Optimal solution is the best feasible solution:
    • The best solution is relative to the OF
    • Sometimes you are looking for the minimums and the smallest value is the best
    • Sometimes you are looking for the maximum solution and the highest is the best
  • Talks specifically about the DVs values:
    • Sometimes the solution is the answer to the question

Example

  • A small furniture company makes end tables and coffee tables. End tables yield 15$ profit and coffee tables yield 25 per table
  • Coffee tables:
    • 6ft of lumber
    • 2 cups of stain
    • 3 labor hours
  • End table:
    • 4ft of lumber
    • 1 cup of stain
    • 1 labor hour
  • Company has(each week):
    • 200 labor hours
    • 480ft of lumber
    • 140 cups of stain
  • How much of each table should each company make

Formulate LP model Step 1 (Find decision variables):

How many of each table to make are our variables \(CT = Coffee tables to produce per-week\) \(ET = End table to produce per-week\)

Step 2 (Objective Function):

Maximize profit \(M = 15ET + 25CT\)

Step 3 (Constraints):

\(Labor = 3CT + ET <= 200hrs\) \(Lumber = 6CT + 4ET <= 480ft\) \(Stain = 2CT + ET <= 140cups\) \(CT >= 0\) \(ET >= 0\)
